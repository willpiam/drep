# Paid Open Source Model (POSM)

The **Paid Open Source Model (POSM)** is Intersect's ecosystem program for funding open source maintenance and contributor support in Cardano. It informed the design of **[[concepts/dospo-omf|dOSPO/OMF]]** and is explicitly referenced as a predecessor whose structural limitations the revised treasury proposal aims to address.

## Position

POSM emerged from open source governance work led by [[entities/christian-taylor|Christian Taylor]] while Head of OSPO at [[entities/intersect|Intersect]]. Programs under the model included:

- **Maintainer Retainer**
- **Tooling Sustainability**
- **Developer Advocates**

These demonstrated **clear demand** for ongoing maintenance funding and operational stewardship. The rollout also exposed that **committees and treasury systems** could approve funding but were **not well suited** to continuous program execution — contributor coordination, lifecycle stewardship, reporting, and delivery — creating friction, delayed implementation, and unclear responsibility boundaries ([[sources/revised-cardano-dospo-and-omf-program-proposal]]; original dOSPO raw motivation).

## Funding path

Treasury funding for POSM flowed through the **Open Source Committee to Intersect MBO** — **not** directly to Christian Taylor or Open Source Cowboy Consulting. The revised dOSPO proposal states its budget is intended to **replace and evolve** that prior work through a more decentralized structure ([[sources/revised-cardano-dospo-and-omf-program-proposal]]).

## Structural criticisms (from dOSPO proposer)

Taylor's dOSPO/OMF materials identify failures in foundation-backed maintainer programs:

| Failure mode | dOSPO/OMF response |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Selection by relationships | Dependency centrality data and published rubrics |
| Continuity funding vulnerable to institutional shifts | Direct treasury withdrawal; DRep replaceable administrator |
| Accountability to foundation board vs community | On-chain reporting; Info Action sunset/replacement |
| Theoretical operator replaceability | Article II.7.5 administrator + explicit Info Action path |
