# Eternl 6 FTE staffing justification

**From query:** 2026-06-13  
**Question:** Does the Eternl proposal say why they need 6 FTE?

## Answer

**No — not in a headcount-specific way.** The proposal states the staffing number and cost math, describes a large scope of work, and splits the budget by function, but it does **not** map roles, hours, or deliverables to justify **6.0 FTE** versus another team size.

### What the proposal does say

| Claim | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Headcount | **10 people** contribute to Eternl; this proposal funds **6.0 FTE** at **$70,000/FTE** |
| Total cost | **$420,000/year** (= 6 × $70k), matching the ₳1,680,000 ask at $0.25/ADA |
| Budget categories | Frontend 55%, Backend 25%, Support 5%, Admin 14%, Audits 1% — dollar shares, not named roles |
| Work scope | Long "Maintenance and Continued Development" section: multi-region backend, four client platforms, governance tooling, support channels, hardfork work, Eternl Core/Hub roadmap |
| Continuity framing | FAQ notes 2025 treasury left them ~**$133k short** and that without funding they would **let go of developers** and shrink to maintenance-only |

The implicit logic is: annual operating cost is $420k → divided by $70k/FTE → **6 FTE**. The detailed workload list explains **what** Eternl maintains and builds, not **why that workload requires exactly six full-time equivalents** (or how the other four of ten contributors are funded).

### What is missing

- No role-by-role table (e.g. 2 frontend, 1 backend, 1 DevOps)
- No hours or sprint capacity model tied to the delivery scope
- No comparison to prior-year staffing under the ₳583k 2025 grant
- No explanation of how the unfunded 4 of 10 contributors are compensated (volunteers, part-time, other revenue)
