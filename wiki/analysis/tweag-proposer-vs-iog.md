# Tweag Treasury Proposer vs IOG / IOR

**From query:** 2026-06-01  
**Question:** Is the Tweag Core Cardano Infrastructure proposal an IOG proposal?

## Answer

**No.** The treasury withdrawal is proposed and delivered by **[[entities/tweag|Tweag by Modus Create]]**, not [[entities/input-output-group|Input Output Group (IOG)]] or [[entities/io-research|IO Research (IOR)]].

| Role | Tweag 2026–2028 | Cardano Vision 2026 (contrast) |
|------|-----------------|--------------------------------|
| **Proposer / contractor** | Tweag by Modus Create | IO Research / IOG |
| **Administrator** | [[entities/intersect|Intersect]] (CDH) | Intersect (CDH) |
| **On-chain metadata author** | Intersect (witness only) | Intersect (typical pattern) |
| **Legal contract party** | Tweag ↔ CDH via Intersect | Input Output ↔ CDH via Intersect |
| **Track** | Engineering delivery (mainnet Peras, History Expiry, testing) | Research program (7 WPs, IOR → ARC → CBU) |

**Why it is often confused with IOG:**

- Tweag has been **continuously engaged by IOG** on Cardano core protocol work since January 2018 (consensus, ledger, Genesis, Peras design).
- The proposal coordinates with IOG via TSC, program Slack, and ecosystem alignment.
- Peras cryptography line items subcontract **CBU/ARK** (IOG research units), but the portfolio owner and primary contractor is Tweag.
- **Intersect** signs the on-chain governance metadata; that does not make Intersect or IOG the proposer.

**Relationship:** Tweag’s proposal is the **engineering complement** to IOR’s Cardano Vision research — see [[concepts/research-delivery-pipeline]]. IOR excludes production hard-fork delivery; Tweag funds that lane separately.
