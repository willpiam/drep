# Intersect 2026: Why Two Separate Proposals

**From query:** 2026-07-08  
**Question:** why are these two proposals separate?

## Answer

They are separated by **scope and governance object**, even though both use Intersect's 2026 treasury administration rails.

[[proposals/intersect-governance-coordination-and-technical-stewardship-2026]] is the **broad operating budget** (₳25.4M) for Intersect-wide functions: MBO operations, governance coordination, technical stewardship, incident response, and a critical-process reserve.

[[proposals/technical-steering-committee-support-2026]] is a **narrow committee-focused budget** (₳1.193M) for TSC-specific work: technical coordination, protocol-governance specialist groups, and an independent technical-review pilot.

In practice, this split gives DReps a way to evaluate/approve/deny a targeted TSC package separately from the much larger Intersect operating ask, rather than forcing one all-or-nothing decision.

## Evidence from proposal text

- TSC proposal explicitly states execution is contingent on approval of the broader IntersectMBO budget, indicating it is a dependent sub-scope rather than a replacement.
- TSC proposal explicitly excludes some adjacent functions (e.g., Security Council and Bug Bounty are funded separately), reinforcing scope isolation.
- Operating proposal groups high-level organizational functions and does not provide a committee-level or person-level compensation roster.

## Uncertainties

- Neither metadata file states an explicit governance rationale sentence like "we split this to improve voting granularity"; that intent is inferred from structure, dependency wording, and separated work-package boundaries.
