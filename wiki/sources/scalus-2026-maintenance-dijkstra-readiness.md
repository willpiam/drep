## Summary

Treasury-withdrawal proposal by **[[entities/lantr-engineering|Lantr Engineering]]** requesting **₳2,464,844** (~$394,375 at $0.16/ADA) over **9 months** (Jul 2026–Mar 2027) for a focused continuation of **[[concepts/scalus|Scalus]]** — JVM-native Cardano application development platform — across maintenance, **[[concepts/dijkstra-hard-fork|Dijkstra]]** readiness, JVM/JS interoperability, and a scoped first **application runtime** release.

This is a reduced resubmission of the prior ₳8.5M / 12-month "Scalus: Cardano's Application Platform" ask. Removed from scope: standalone JVM L1 node, full Gummiworm L2 integration, broad formal verification, advanced devnet expansion. Administration uses **[[entities/sundae-labs|Sundae Labs]]** treasury-contract escrow with an independent oversight board (Gianelloni, Benkort, Kilgore) and No.Witness Labs technical assurance.

## Key points

- **Traction:** 3 years continuous delivery; 4,642 commits / 32 releases; components embedded in MeshJS, Lucid Evolution, Evolution SDK, Cardano Client Lib, YaciDevKit; 20k+/mo Scalus.js npm downloads.
- **Direct adopters:** Gummiworm L2, Bifrost, SugarRush DEX, Vela stablecoin, DID/DIDComm.
- **Three workstreams:** Protect (maintenance + Dijkstra preview), Deepen (JVM/Java/Kotlin + JS/TS reuse), Expand (scoped application runtime: chain follower, scheduling, persistence, reactive workers).
- **Dijkstra scope:** Plutus V4, nested transactions, accounts, guard script types — developer-preview ahead of hard fork, conformance testing through M3.
- **Budget discipline:** 2.25 FTE over 9 months; 0% contingency; conservative $0.16/ADA; partial stablecoin hedge planned (lesson from 2025 ADA depreciation).
- **Prior funding disclosed:** Catalyst F11/F13 grants; ₳657,692 2025 Treasury Budget (completed, all milestones delivered).
- **Independent submission:** Bypasses bundled budget action; quarterly milestones with more outcome-focused flexibility than 2025 Intersect-facilitated contract.
