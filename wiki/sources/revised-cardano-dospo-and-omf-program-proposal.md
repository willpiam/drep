# Revised Cardano dOSPO and OMF Program Proposal

**Raw:** [../raw/Revised-Cardano-dOSPO-and-OMF-Program-Proposal.json](../raw/Revised-Cardano-dOSPO-and-OMF-Program-Proposal.json)  
**Ingested:** 2026-07-12

## Summary

[[entities/christian-taylor|Christian Taylor]] / Open Source Cowboy Consulting requests a **12-month treasury withdrawal of ₳4,094,000** to pilot a **[[concepts/dospo-omf|decentralized Open Source Program Office (dOSPO)]]** and **Open Maintenance Framework (OMF)** — an independent operational layer for sustaining Cardano's open source infrastructure as public goods.

This is a **revised, shortened successor** to the original **Cardano dOSPO and OMF Program** (₳12M / 36 months; raw: [bafybeialmkgxruw4oigiyv77ixm6yjjrjfh55danwq2u46hqwo4flicrhi.json](../raw/bafybeialmkgxruw4oigiyv77ixm6yjjrjfh55danwq2u46hqwo4flicrhi.json)), which attracted >98% NO votes before expiry ([vote 57](../../vote_context/markdown/57_dOSPO.md)). The revision cuts duration and budget, explicitly names an **Article II.7.5 administrator at withdrawal**, and reframes advisory councils as **non-vetoing** transparency bodies.

**Administrator:** Christian Taylor / Open Source Cowboy Consulting — sole Article II.7.5 funds administrator from treasury withdrawal through the 12-month term, unless replaced by on-chain DRep Info Action. Proposer commits to sponsoring any such Info Action requested by **15+ DReps**.

**Auditor:** Mill Law Firm — quarterly financial reviews and annual audit; reports attached to quarterly outcome reports.

**Legal entity:** US **501(c)(3) public charity** targeted by month 6 (M1.2); transition of administrator role to the entity requires a separate on-chain Info Action.

Five work packages deliver four programs: **Maintenance Fund** (dependency-data retainers), **Maintainer Development** (mentor–mentee pipelines with on-chain attestations), **CodeForUs** (bounties + tooling sustainability), and **Ecosystem Activation Reserve** (Summer of Code, hackathons). Selection uses published dependency centrality and bus-factor data, not relationships. Undeployed reserves (₳175k WP2 portfolio, ₳44k WP5 activation, operational contingency) return to treasury.

The proposal builds on lessons from Intersect's **[[concepts/paid-open-source-model|Paid Open Source Model (POSM)]]** — Maintainer Retainer, Tooling Sustainability, Developer Advocates — which received treasury funding through the Open Source Committee to Intersect MBO, not to Taylor personally. Taylor previously built Intersect's OSPO function and departed to pursue a governance-independent structure.

## Key points

- **Total ask:** ₳4,094,000 over 12 months (vs ₳12M / 36 months in original)
- **WP1 Operations:** ₳760,000 — lean team (2 FTE + 2 PT), advisory councils (1,000 ADA/member/month), 501(c)(3) formation
- **WP2 Maintenance Fund:** ₳2,000,000 — dependency audit, SBOMs, three retainer tiers, CHAOSS dashboard
- **WP3 Maintainer Development:** ₳1,000,000 — Core + Tooling tracks, contribution ladder, stake-key attestations
- **WP4 CodeForUs:** ₳167,000 — bounties (30k cap) + tooling sustainability stream
- **WP5 Ecosystem Activation:** ₳167,000 — Cardano Summer of Code, 2 hackathons, conversion funnel to WP3/WP4
- **Governance:** Two advisory councils (External OS + Technical Community) publish written feedback before each disbursement; administrator holds final allocation authority within published parameters
- **Replaceability:** DReps may pass Info Action to replace administrator or sunset program; early termination returns uncommitted funds within 30 days
- **Overlap avoidance:** Proposer commits to deliberate non-overlap with existing funding programs
- **GTM angle:** Help projects (e.g. Charli3, Taptools) transition toward commercial open source where appropriate
- **Whitepapers:** dOSPO v1.0 and OMF v1.0 (March 2026) — opensourcecowboy.com; co-developed with Bitergia, CHAOSS, ASF, LFDT, Intersect, Andamio, Modus Create, others
- **Strategic pillars:** 1 (Infrastructure), 4 (Community growth), 5 (Sustainability & resilience)
- **KPIs:** 40% top-50 dependencies funded by month 12; bus factor 2+ for top-20; <15% maintainer attrition; <48h security response; 100% quarterly transparency
