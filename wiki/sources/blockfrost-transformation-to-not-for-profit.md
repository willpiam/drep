# Blockfrost's transformation to not-for-profit

**Raw:** [../raw/Blockfrost's-transformation-to-not-for-profit.json](../raw/Blockfrost's-transformation-to-not-for-profit.json)  
**Ingested:** 2026-07-10

## Summary

[[entities/input-output-group|Input Output Global]] and [[entities/intersect|Intersect]] (co-author witness) request **₳9,832,979** (~**$1,868,266** at **$0.19/ADA**) over **18 months** to transition **[[entities/blockfrost|Blockfrost]]** from an IOG-held commercial service into a **community-governed not-for-profit public API**. The ask funds legal entity formation (or integration with an existing host such as PRAGMA), technical migration, a preliminary board handoff to an elected five-seat board, and sustained operations with quarterly reporting and a public usage dashboard.

Blockfrost is positioned as critical Cardano developer infrastructure: the #1 hosted platform in the Cardano Foundation developer survey (39.3% in 2022 → **71.5% in 2025**), serving **~781k unique visitors** and **1.84B API requests** monthly (~700 rps, 7 TB data). The proposer claims **>50% of mainnet transactions** are submitted via Blockfrost in most epochs and **90% of API traffic** uses the free tier — making a dual commercial/public-good model unsustainable without harming adoption. An earlier treasury ask was **not funded**; feedback cited hesitation about treasury backing a commercial venture, motivating this fully not-for-profit redesign.

Governance: five-seat board (four open-source infrastructure-entity seats + one community seat); elections targeted by end of 2026, seated by end of Q1 2027. Preliminary board: Beatrice Anihiri (Blockfrost), Christina Gianelloni (BlinkLabs), Federico Weill (TxPipe), Jeni Davidson (SundaeLabs), Francis Luz (Masumi/Begin Wallet). IOG holds a non-voting advisory seat for year one. All IP (source, trademarks, domains) transfers to the NFP.

Long-term sustainability (post-18-month subsidy) is left to the elected board — options include **NFP-operated paid tiers** with profits returned to treasury, or a **vendor-backed federation** where official partners serve commercial needs and may co-operate infrastructure (including existing **[[concepts/blockfrost-decentralization|Icebreakers]]**). Unspent post-transition infrastructure budget returns to treasury.

Administration follows the standard IOG/CDH pattern: legal contract, Intersect TRSC/PSSC ([[entities/sundae-labs|Sundae Labs]] framework, **2026 TRSC**), Oversight Committee multisig, 3rd-party assurer, milestone metadata on-chain, [treasury.sundae.fi](https://treasury.sundae.fi) dashboard. Prior IO Blockfrost workstream receipt: **₳1,137,500** (88% of prior allocation).

## Key points

- **Budget (18 months):** Staffing 79.1% (₳7,780,347 / ~$1.48M — 6 FTE: PM, community manager, 4 devs); Ops & infrastructure 19.3% (₳1,894,737 / ~$360k, ~$20k/mo stack); Legal & accounting 1.6% (₳157,895 / ~$30k).
- **Milestones:** Q3 2026 entity + dashboard; Q4 2026 on-chain board election; Q1 2027 full traffic cutover + IP transfer; Q2–Q3 2027 sustainability consultation; 2027 ops milestones with **99% monthly uptime SLA** and quarterly reports.
- **Decentralization context:** Founded 2020 (Five Binaries); joined IOG 2024 to decentralize; **100+ Icebreakers** already operating.
- **Vision 2030 alignment:** Claims direct/indirect enablement of transactions, MAU, TVL, protocol revenue, throughput scaling; full alignment cited for Infrastructure, Adoption, and Ecosystem Sustainability pillars.
- **Refund:** Unspent funds at delivery end returned; proportional refund on scope reduction; NCL TREASURY-02a compliant at submission.
- **On-chain:** Treasury withdrawal to 2026 TRSC stake `stake1784sdxt6jjennmstphgdu7l7c2scf5d02a6cve2dgn5s2kq5u3j9v` — ₳9,832,979.
