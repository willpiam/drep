## Summary

$computerman voted **YES** on Tweag's treasury withdrawal for **₳39,787,316** over **2026–2028** to deliver **17 work packages** of core L1 infrastructure (Peras mainnet path, History Expiry, testing scaffolding, and related tooling). The published rationale acknowledges widespread **NO** sentiment tied to the **two-year horizon** and **single bundled package**, but argues both can be appropriate when the proposer states they need continuous multi-year delivery and an integrated pipeline.

## Key points

- **Vote:** YES (William Doyle / $computerman).
- **Ask recap:** ₳39.8M; 17 WPs; aligns with [[sources/tweag-core-cardano-infrastructure-2026-2028]] and [[analysis/tweag-2026-2028-work-package-deliverables]].
- **Community objections noted:** Dislike of 2-year timeline; dislike of bundling multiple workstreams into one governance action.
- **Bundling:** Prior skepticism of bundled treasury proposals has softened — bundling can make sense when work packages are interdependent (as Tweag and the wiki proposal page argue for Peras + testing + History Expiry).
- **Governance granularity:** The DRep process may need to operate at a **higher level of abstraction** than line-item votes; the ecosystem is still calibrating the right detail level for democratic control.
- **Two-year timeline:** Defensible if Tweag believes **two years of continuous development** are required to produce results; proposer-stated success conditions should weigh in delegate judgment.
- **Proposer deference:** When the contractor communicates conditions they need to succeed, delegates should take that into account rather than substituting a preferred decomposition.
