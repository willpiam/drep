# IO Research (IOR)

**Input Output Research** is the scientific research arm of [[entities/input-output-group|Input Output Group]], responsible for peer-reviewed protocol research, formal methods, and technology validation that feeds Cardano engineering. It leads the **[[concepts/cardano-vision|Cardano Vision]]** treasury-funded program.

## Role

IOR designs and proves new protocols (consensus, cryptography, tokenomics, governance), then hands validated work to **ARC** (Applied Research & Creative Engineering) for prototypes and to **CBU** (Cardano Business Unit) for product alignment. The [[concepts/research-delivery-pipeline|integrated delivery model]] uses TRL staging: fundamental research (≤TRL2), technology validation (TRL3–5), implementation support (TRL5+).

## Track record (per Cardano Vision 2026 proposal)

- 250+ peer-reviewed papers since inception; ~20% implemented in Cardano
- Cardano Vision 2025 approved: ₳4.16B yes stake (79.81%)
- 2025: 20 research streams, 24 papers (+20% vs target); outputs include Peras/Leios proofs, bridge security framework, Cavefish light client, AGATE/UC-SNARKs, Phalanx, Jolteon, RSnarks feasibility
- Prior treasury workstream (IOR): ₳26.84M allocated; 100% of that allocation assigned

## Cardano Vision 2026

See [[proposals/cardano-vision-2026]] and [[sources/cardano-vision-2026-ior]]. Chief Scientist **Aggelos Kiayias** (Ouroboros architect) oversees research strategy. Consortium includes Edinburgh, Science Tokyo, Buenos Aires, Sydney, Berkeley, Oxford, ZHAW, Eryx, and others.
